How a review is performed

Each review follows a defined, document-based process designed to provide clear, repeatable outcomes while maintaining strict boundaries on scope and interpretation.

  1. Scope definition Before any documents are reviewed, the scope of the engagement is established. This includes the checklist applied, the document categories covered, and any explicit inclusions or exclusions. The scope is fixed prior to review and is not adjusted based on document content or findings.
  2. Document submission Documents are submitted through a controlled channel and logged upon receipt. Each submission is assigned a reference identifier and review date for traceability. Only documents provided within the defined scope are considered as part of the review.
  3. Document review Documents are reviewed on a document-only basis. The review is limited to confirming document presence, noting visible internal consistency, and recording stated validity or expiry information where available. No verification of authenticity, accuracy, safety, or regulatory compliance is performed.
  4. Summary issuance Upon completion of the review, a Safety Readiness Summary (PDF) is prepared. The summary records the documents reviewed, identifies missing, expired, or unclear items, and documents observations strictly within the defined scope. All limitations and disclaimers are explicitly stated in the summary.
  5. Retention and deletion Submitted documents are retained only for the period required to complete the review. After completion, all documents are deleted in accordance with the stated retention window. Review logs containing reference identifiers and processing metadata are preserved.
